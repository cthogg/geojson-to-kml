St Anne's Hospital ( Q12016624 )


ano
   
26304/7-170
Memory catalog
MIS Old Brno Pekařská 664/53 and 57, Anenská 664/15, Leitnerova
49°11′26.86″ N , 16°35′54.02″ E Hospital at St. Anne. A monumental Neo-Renaissance work by Theophilus von Hansen from the 1860s, situated in a park-like environment. The hospital complex was built on the site of the demolished buildings of the former Dominican convent of medieval foundation (beginning of the 14th century), which served as a hospital and nursing home since the Josephine reforms. The monumental building complex, centered around the trapezoidal courtyard, consists of the main front wing with a massive central elevation with an entrance vestibule and two other wings connected at a slight angle, from which two long courtyard wings extend at right angles and end in short wings connected at right angles. These wings are connected to each other by a ground-floor arcade corridor with the unique Neo-Palladian architecture of the institutional chapel of St. Anne in the middle. The elevated central risalit of the main wing is significantly pushed out into the courtyard area in the courtyard frontage, so it resembles a short wing.
  • Neo-Palladian Chapel of St. Anne's central arrangement on a Greek cross plan with a vestibule and a semicircular recessed apse arched with a conch, above the nave of a square plan is a tambour bearing a dome surmounted by an open lantern with a domed canopy. Tribunes on beams carried by Doric columns are inserted into the side naves.
  • The original park, which surrounded the hospital premises from the west and especially the east side and which was founded on the site of the original garden of the Dominican nuns, has largely disappeared today in the extensive construction of hospital buildings continuously built from the end of the 19th century to the present day.

Listed since March 23, 1964.
